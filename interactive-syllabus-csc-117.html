Spring 2026 | Asynchronous Online

Catalog Description

Introduction to Programming and Computational Thinking using Python serves as a first programming course for Networking & Cybersecurity majors. This course is for beginning programmers. The course emphasizes the development of languages and software, problem-solving, and programming in a structured, object-oriented language. The Python programming language is used throughout the course.

Course Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, you will be able to:

  • Design computer algorithms to solve problems.
  • Create and document computer programs using the formal syntax from a high-level, object-oriented programming language.
  • Adopt an iterative and continuous improvement process to critically troubleshoot issues and elevate software design.

Method of Evaluation

This course is graded on a point system. Your final grade is computed by summing the points you earn and dividing by the total number of possible points.

This Python course has frequent assignments (quizzes, labs, projects, etc.) with clear expectations and due dates. Due to the course's fast pace, late submissions are discouraged. Course content is interconnected; understanding early concepts is crucial for later topics. You may submit labs and projects as often as you wish (up to the due date).

If you are struggling, please seek help promptly! You can meet with me, email me, or use provided resources like GitHub, YouTube videos, or the FLCC Discord.

Submission Guidelines

Assignments will be submitted via Brightspace or GitHub. All scores will be entered in Brightspace, where you can monitor your grade. GitHub may involve autotesting to help you check your work before final submission.

Late Work Policy

Every assignment has an automatic one-week grace period (with the exception of time-sensitive Discussions). If you don’t hand something in on the due date, you have one more week to submit it.

Exception: No work is accepted after the semester ends. The final project does not have a grace period.

Always back up your work in multiple locations (e.g., Google Docs, external drive). Lost assignments will receive a grade of zero.

Attendance & Participation

As this is an online course, you choose when to work. Successful students typically engage with coursework at least 3 times per week. Never attending or stopping attendance may impact financial aid eligibility.

Participation includes:

  • Engaging in discussions.
  • Completing assignments to the best of your ability.
  • Engaging with course material and other learners.
  • Asking questions when you need help!

Academic Integrity & AI Policy

Students are expected to read, understand, and internalize the college policy on cheating and plagiarism. Academic dishonesty is not tolerated and may result in a grade of "F" for the course.

AI Usage: If you use any AI tools (like ChatGPT) in this course, you must disclose your use. For Python code, disclose it in comments. Do NOT use AI tools for your weekly reflections, which should be your own thoughts.

Classroom Decorum

Discussion in this class will always value contrasting opinions and constructive conversation. Offensive comments, poor attitudes, and charged statements will not be tolerated.
